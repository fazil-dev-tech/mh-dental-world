Common Dental Emergencies

If you're experiencing any of these, contact us immediately.

😣

Severe Toothache

Persistent, throbbing pain that doesn't get better with over-the-counter painkillers. Could indicate infection or abscess.

🦷

Knocked-Out Tooth

Time is critical! Keep the tooth moist (in milk or saliva) and get to us within 30 minutes for the best chance of reimplantation.

💥

Broken/Chipped Tooth

Rinse with warm water, apply cold compress. Save any fragments. We can repair or replace the damaged tooth.

🩸

Uncontrolled Bleeding

Bleeding that doesn't stop after extraction or injury. Apply pressure with gauze and come in immediately.

😷

Swelling/Abscess

Facial swelling, pus discharge, or fever with dental pain. This could be a serious infection requiring immediate treatment.

👑

Lost Crown/Filling

A lost crown or filling exposes your tooth to further damage and sensitivity. We can re-cement or replace it quickly.

What To Do in a Dental Emergency

1

Stay Calm

Take a deep breath. Most dental emergencies can be treated effectively with prompt care.

2

Call Us Immediately

Call +91 98456 61301. Describe your situation so we can prepare for your arrival.

3
